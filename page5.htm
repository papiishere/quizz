Limitations or demerits of project based learning method

Frame 41: Financial constraints: The execution of projects demands substantial financial resources, such as purchasing seeds, plants, and other necessities. Returns may not always justify the expenditure, making it impractical for many institutions due to budget limitations.
Execution of project may lead to ____________

Answer: ( financial issues

Frame 42: Time-consuming: Projects require extensive planning and execution, often spanning years for completion. It’s challenging to cover the entire curriculum through projects within a single session, making it impractical for comprehensive learning.
Time consuming project are often impractical for ______________________

Answer: comprehensive learning

Frame 43 : High expectations from teachers: Successful project implementation places heavy demands on teachers, from facilitating project selection to providing guidance throughout. Teachers need to possess diverse knowledge and skills, making it a challenging task.
Teachers are expected to possess diverse ______________ and __________ for successful project implementation

Answer: knowledge and skill

Frame 44 : Limited systematic learning: Project-based learning may result in fragmented knowledge acquisition, as students focus primarily on project completion rather than systematic learning. This method lacks structured teaching approaches, raising concerns about its effectiveness in providing organized learning experiences.
Fragmented knowledge acquisition occurs when students don’t focus on _______________ learning.

Answer: systematic

Frame 44 : Practical difficulties: Schools often lack the necessary funds and infrastructure for project implementation. The vastness of social sciences curricula and the mismatch with examination systems further hinder the effective use of this method. Effective use of project method can often be hinder by lack of ______________ and _______________

Answer: funds , infrastructure
